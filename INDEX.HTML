Welcome to araku

Aaraku Valley, located in the Eastern Ghats of Andhra Pradesh, India, is a serene and picturesque hill station known for its stunning landscapes, coffee plantations, and rich tribal culture. Situated about 114 kilometers from Visakhapatnam at an altitude of around 3,000 feet, the valley offers a cool, temperate climate that provides respite from the hot coastal plains. Aaraku is especially famous for its organic coffee plantations, where Arabica and Robusta coffee beans are cultivated. The region's coffee is renowned for its high quality, rich taste, and aromatic appeal, and it has gained recognition both domestically and internationally. Coffee tourism is a significant part of the local economy, with visitors often touring estates to learn about the coffee-making process and enjoy freshly brewed coffee.

Aaraku is easily accessible by road from Visakhapatnam, and the journey itself is a scenic experience, passing through winding roads and dense forests. The valley’s climate, with temperatures ranging from 18°C to 25°C, makes it an ideal destination throughout the year, though the best time to visit is between October and March. The region's emphasis on sustainable farming and its promotion of organic coffee production have made it a model for eco-tourism and agricultural sustainability. With its blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and unique tourism experiences, Aaraku Valley is rapidly becoming a must-visit destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those interested in experiencing the charm of rural India.
